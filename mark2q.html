Section 1: Foundations of Consumer Behavior (Questions 1-20)

  1. What is the primary definition of "Consumer Behavior" given in the slides?

    Consumer behavior refers to the actions and decisions that people make when they are purchasing or using products.

  2. What are the four key activities highlighted in the more detailed definition of consumer behavior?

    The four key activities are: choose, buy, use, and dispose of a product or service.

  3. Define "Consumer Buyer Behavior."

    It is the buying behavior of final consumersâ€”individuals and households that buy goods and services for personal consumption.

  4. How does the study of consumer behavior help understand how individuals spend their resources?

    It is the study of how individuals make decisions to spend their available resources (like time, money, effort) on consumption-related items.

  5. What aspect of consumer behavior did Loudon & Bitta (1993) emphasize in their definition?

    They emphasized the "decision process and physical activities" individuals engage in when evaluating, acquiring, using, or disposing of goods and services.

  6. What did Peter (1996) describe as the "dynamic interaction" in consumer behavior?

    He described it as the dynamic interaction of affect and cognition, behavior, and environmental events by which human beings conduct the exchange aspect of their lives.

  7. How did Hawkins (2005) expand the scope of consumer behavior studies?

    Hawkins expanded the scope to include not just individuals but also groups and organizations, and looked at the entire process they use to select, secure, use, and dispose of products, services, experiences, or ideas.

  8. What are the six Intended Learning Outcomes (ILOs) of this lesson?

    1. Define the consumer market. 2. Understand Consumer Behavior as a Process. 3. Understand Consumer roles. 4. Understand Major types of consumers. 5. The Customer Development Process. 6. Consumer Buying Process.

  9. Why is it important for marketers to know "which people to target"?

    To ensure marketing efforts are focused on the most receptive and profitable audience, avoiding wasted resources on those who are unlikely to buy.

  10. Why must marketers understand "where to reach" their target audience?

    To select the most effective marketing channels (e.g., social media, TV, retail stores) where their target audience spends their time.

  11. What role does consumer behavior play in deciding "what messages to communicate"?

    Understanding consumer motivations, needs, and values helps craft messages that resonate with the audience and persuade them to act.

  12. How does studying consumer behavior help in choosing "what media to use"?

    It reveals the media consumption habits of the target audience, allowing marketers to place ads on the platforms they use most frequently.

  13. Explain how consumer behavior insights influence the choice of "distribution method."

    Insights into where and how consumers prefer to shop (e.g., online, in-store, through subscription) determine the most effective distribution channels.

  14. Why is understanding "differences between groups" a key part of studying consumer behavior?

    It is the foundation of market segmentation, allowing marketers to tailor products and messages to different groups instead of using a one-size-fits-all approach.

  15. How does consumer behavior help marketers learn "how to keep customers"?

    By understanding post-purchase behavior, satisfaction drivers, and loyalty factors, marketers can develop effective retention strategies.

  16. What are the two major types of consumers?

    1. Personal Consumers (individuals/households buying for personal use). 2. Organizational Consumers (businesses, non-profits, government agencies buying for operational purposes).

  17. Who is considered an "end user"?

    An end user is another term for a personal consumer who purchases products for their own use.

  18. List three types of Organizational Consumers.

    Business organizations, Non-profit organizations, and Government agencies (also institutional buyers).

  19. What does the tip "Know Your Customers" entail for a marketer?

    It means using modern tools to identify who their customers are (and are not) and using this data to improve marketing with targeted ads and coupons.

  20. Explain the tip "Work with Retailers."

    It means collaborating with stores to connect manufacturer needs with retailer needs, and adjusting marketing and promotions for each specific store environment.

Section 2: Consumer Behavior as a Process (Questions 21-45)

  1. What is the key consumer question during the "Pre-purchase Issues" stage?

    "How does a consumer decide about needing a product?"

  2. What is the key marketer question during the "Pre-purchase Issues" stage?

    "How are consumer attitudes formed/changed?"

  3. What is the key consumer question during the "Purchase Issues" stage?

    "Is product acquisition a stressful or pleasant experience?"

  4. What is the key marketer question during the "Purchase Issues" stage?

    "How do situational factors affect purchase decision?"

  5. What are the key consumer questions during the "Post-purchase Issues" stage?

    "Does the product provide pleasure or perform its function? How is the product disposed of?"

  6. What is the key marketer question during the "Post-purchase Issues" stage?

    "What determines customer satisfaction and repurchase?"

  7. Define the consumer role of the "consumer (user)."

    The person who consumes or uses the product.

  8. Define the consumer role of the "buyer."

    The person who undertakes activities to procure or obtain the product.

  9. Define the consumer role of the "payer."

    The person who provides the money (or other value) to obtain the product.

  10. Provide an example where the buyer, payer, and user are three different people.

    A company manager (buyer) uses the company credit card (payer = the company) to purchase a software license for an employee (user).

  11. What is "Affect" in the context of consumer analysis?

    Affect refers to the internal psychological responses related to feelings, such as positive or negative emotions like love or anger.

  12. What is "Cognition" in the context of consumer analysis?

    Cognition refers to the internal psychological responses related to thinking, such as knowledge structures and mental processes.

  13. What does "Behavior" refer to in consumer analysis?

    It refers to the overt acts or actions of consumers, such as buying, visiting a store, or watching TV commercials.

  14. What does "Environment" refer to in consumer analysis?

    It refers to the complex of physical and social stimuli in the external world (things, places, people) that influence a consumer's affect, cognition, and behavior.

  15. How does the "Wheel of Consumer Analysis" show the relationship between its three elements and marketing strategy?

    It shows that marketing strategy is at the center, and it must be developed with a deep understanding of the dynamic, reciprocal relationship between Consumer Behavior, the Consumer Environment, and Affect & Cognition. A change in one element affects the others.

  16. Describe the first stage of the Consumer Buying Process: Problem Recognition.

    This is the first step where a person notices a difference between their desired state and their actual state, creating a need or a problem to solve. For example, running out of a product or wanting a better version.

  17. Describe the second stage of the Consumer Buying Process: Information Search.

    After recognizing a need, consumers begin looking for information to help them decide. This can involve searching online, asking friends, reading reviews, or paying attention to ads.

  18. Describe the third stage of the Consumer Buying Process: Evaluation of Alternatives.

    At this stage, consumers use the information they gathered to look at different options or brands. They compare the advantages and disadvantages of each and narrow down their choices.

  19. Describe the fourth stage of the Consumer Buying Process: Purchase Decision.

    After comparing their options, the consumer decides what to buy. This choice can be influenced by factors like price, availability, discounts, and personal preferences.

  20. Describe the fifth stage of the Consumer Buying Process: Post-Purchase Evaluation.

    After the purchase, consumers think about whether they are happy with their choice. They assess if the product met their expectations. If satisfied, they may buy again; if not, they might return it or leave a negative review.

  21. What is "Post-Purchase Behavior"?

    It is the stage where customers engage in behaviors after the purchase, which may include sharing their experiences through reviews, referring friends, or seeking customer support.

Section 3: Models and Application (Questions 46-60)

  1. In the "Simple Model of Consumer Decision Making," what are the two main types of "External Influences"?

    1. Firm's Marketing Efforts (Product, Promotion, Price, Channels of distribution). 2. Sociocultural Environment (Family, informal sources, social class, culture).

  2. What are the three core steps in the "Consumer Decision Making" part of the process stage?

    1. Need Recognition. 2. Prepurchase Search. 3. Evaluation of Alternatives.

  3. List the five factors in the "Psychological Field" that influence the process.

    1. Motivation. 2. Perception. 3. Learning. 4. Personality. 5. Attitudes.

  4. How does "Experience" bridge the gap between the process and output stages?

    The consumer's experience during and after the purchase directly informs the "Postpurchase Evaluation" and also feeds back into the "Psychological Field" (e.g., learning, changing attitudes) to influence future decisions.

  5. What are the two components of the "Output" or "Postdecision Behavior" stage?

    1. Purchase (Trial, Repeat purchase). 2. Postpurchase Evaluation.

  6. How did the recognition of consumer behavior (CB) evolve alongside marketing philosophies?

    Early philosophies (Production, Product, Selling) had little focus on CB. The shift to the Marketing Concept made understanding CB central to strategy. The Societal Marketing Concept further deepened this by considering the long-term impacts of consumer behavior on society.

  7. How does the "Societal Marketing" philosophy relate to consumer behavior?

    It considers the long-term welfare implications of consumer behavior, aiming to satisfy needs in a way that is good for both the individual consumer and society as a whole.

  8. What is the "consumer market"?

    The consumer market consists of all individuals and households who buy or acquire goods and services for personal consumption.

  9. Why is consumer behavior considered a "process"?

    Because it's not a single act of buying, but a series of stages that occur before, during, and after the purchase.

  10. What is an example of a "situational factor" that could affect a purchase decision?

    Examples could be the store atmosphere, time pressure, the consumer's mood, or the presence of a persuasive salesperson.

  11. How is product "disposal" a part of consumer behavior?

    It is the final phase of the product's life with the consumer. How they dispose of it (e.g., recycling, reselling, throwing away) has environmental and social implications and is a key area of study.

  12. What does the marketing tip "Make What They Want" imply?

    It implies that product development should be driven by consumer research and insights into what people actually like and need, rather than what the company thinks they should have.

  13. Explain "Use Targeted and New Media" as a marketing tip.

    This means moving beyond traditional mass media to advertise on specific channels that the target audience uses, like niche cable TV channels, special interest magazines, or modern digital platforms.

  14. What does "Reach Customers in the Store" mean?

    It refers to in-store marketing efforts that influence the final purchase decision at the point of sale, such as ads on shopping carts, in-store radio, or shelf displays.

  15. Explain the tip "Sharpen Your Promotions."

    This advises marketers to make promotions simple and effective rather than overly complex or expensive. Promotions should be well-matched to the brand and can be used to target a competitor's customers.
