WHAT IS PRICING?

Pricing is the process of determining the monetary value of a product or service. It is a critical aspect of marketing that affects profitability and market share.

Importance of Pricing

  • Influences customer purchasing decisions.
  • Impacts business profitability.
  • Reflects brand positioning and value proposition.

Different Views on Price

Economist's view Price is set by the forces of supply and demand.
Accountant's view Price should cover costs so that a profit can be made.
Customer's view Price has to represent good value.
Marketer's view Pricing is an opportunity to gain a competitive advantage.

PRICE CONSIDERATION

Pricing decisions are influenced by both internal company factors and external environmental factors.

Internal Factors

  • Marketing Objectives
  • Marketing Strategies
  • Other elements of the marketing mix

External Factors

  • Nature of the market
  • Nature of the demand
  • Competitor's strategy
  • Other environmental factors

PROCESS OF PRICING

1. Setting the Pricing Objectives
2. Determining Demand
3. Estimating Costs
4. Analyzing Competitor's Pricing
5. Selecting Pricing Method
6. Selecting Final Price

Step 1: Selecting Pricing Objectives

A company can select any of the following objectives:

  • Survival
  • Maximize current profits
  • Maximize market share
  • Market skimming
  • Product quality leadership
  • Partial cost recovery
  • Customer retention or relationship building

Step 2: Determine the Demand

Concentrate on the demand conditions and the elasticity of demand. Consumers are generally less price sensitive when:

  • The product is more distinctive.
  • Buyers are less aware of substitutes.
  • The expenditure is a small part of the buyer's total income.
  • The product is used with assets previously bought.

Step 3: Estimating Cost

Key cost considerations include:

  • Types of costs (fixed, variable) and levels of production.
  • Accumulated production leads to cost reduction (the experience curve).
  • Differentiated marketing offers create different cost levels.

Step 4: Analyzing Competitor's Cost, Price & Offerings

Firms must analyze the competition with respect to their costs, prices, and possible reactions to price changes. Pricing decisions are also influenced by the quality of your offering relative to the competition.

SELECTING PRICING METHOD

The Three "C's" of Pricing

Price-setting begins with consideration of three key factors:

  • Cost: Cost-oriented pricing (e.g., cost-plus).
  • Customer: Customer-oriented pricing (e.g., value-based).
  • Competition: Competitor-oriented pricing (e.g., going-rate).

New Product Pricing Strategies

Market Skimming

Setting a high initial price for a new product to "skim" maximum revenue from segments willing to pay the high price. The company makes fewer but more profitable sales.

Example: Apple iPhones, Sony PlayStation consoles at launch.

Market Penetration

Setting a low initial price for a new product to attract a large number of buyers quickly and win a large market share.

Example: Netflix, Walmart, AliExpress.

Price Adjustment Strategies

Strategy Description
Discount & Allowance Pricing Reducing prices to reward customer responses (e.g., paying early).
Segmented Pricing Adjusting prices for different customers, products, or locations.
Psychological Pricing Adjusting prices for psychological effect (e.g., $9.99 vs. $10.00).
Promotional Pricing Temporarily reducing prices for short-run sales.
Dynamic Pricing Adjusting prices continually to meet the needs of individual customers.

CHALLENGES & FUTURE TRENDS

Challenges in Pricing

  • Balancing Profitability and Demand: Too high may deter sales; too low may reduce profits.
  • Managing Customer Expectations: Communicating price changes effectively.

Future Trends in Pricing

  • AI-Driven Pricing: Using machine learning for real-time price optimization.
  • Sustainability Pricing: Reflecting environmental costs in pricing strategies.
