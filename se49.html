01 WHAT IS MARKETING

What It Is and Why It Matters to You

Defining Marketing

Marketing?

The art and science of creating, communicating, and delivering value to satisfy the needs of a target market.

Marketing Management?

Coordination of strategic planning, implementation, and control of activities designed to bring about desired exchanges with target markets.

"Marketing is the process by which companies create value for customers and build strong customer relationships in order to capture value in return." - Philip Kotler

Marketing vs. Selling & Advertising

The Iceberg Concept

What people often see (selling and advertising) is just the "tip of the iceberg." The majority of marketing work—research, strategy, product development, pricing—happens below the surface.

Advertising vs. Marketing

Advertising is a component of marketing, not the whole thing. It is a subset focused specifically on communicating a promotional message to a target audience.

Advertising Scope Includes:

  • TV/radio commercials
  • Print ads (newspapers, magazines)
  • Online ads (social media, Google, banners)
  • Outdoor ads (billboards, transit)
  • Influencer or celebrity endorsements

Core Marketing Concepts

Marketing revolves around a core set of interconnected concepts that form a continuous cycle.

Needs vs. Wants

Understanding the difference between needs and wants is fundamental to marketing.

Aspect Needs Wants
Definition Essentials required for survival or basic functioning. Desires or luxuries that enhance comfort or satisfaction.
Examples Food, water, shelter, healthcare, basic clothing. Latest gadgets, designer clothes, vacations.
Fulfillment Priority High - necessary for maintaining life and health. Low - optional and based on personal choice.
Flexibility Non-negotiable and fundamental human requirement. Flexible and subject to personal preferences.
Change Will remain constant over time. May change over time.

Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

This psychological theory helps marketers understand consumer motivation at different levels.

Products, Services & Offerings

Marketers provide offerings to satisfy needs and wants. These can be tangible goods or intangible services.

Goods (Tangible)

A product is a tangible item that is created, manufactured, and sold to fulfill a need or want.
e.g., a car, a shirt, food.

Services (Intangible)

A service is an intangible activity or benefit offered to fulfill a need, usually produced and consumed at the same time.
e.g., a haircut, legal advice.

Value, Exchange, and Satisfaction

Customer Value

Value is the customer's perception of what they get versus what they give up.

Customer Value = Perceived Benefits - Perceived Costs

  • What they get (Benefits): quality, features, comfort, experience.
  • What they give up (Costs): money, time, effort.

Customer Satisfaction

Satisfaction is how a customer feels after using a product/service and comparing it to their expectations. Setting the right expectations is crucial.

  • Performance = Expectations ➡️ Satisfied
  • Performance < Expectations ➡️ Dissatisfied
  • Performance > Expectations ➡️ Delighted

Exchange and Markets

An exchange is the core of marketing—obtaining a desired product by offering something of value in return. This happens in a market, which is any platform where buyers and sellers interact.

  • Market Place: A physical location (e.g., a retail store).
  • Market Space: A digital location (e.g., an e-commerce website).
  • Meta Market: A cluster of related goods and services (e.g., a "wedding market" including venues, caterers, photographers).

The Marketing Mix

The Traditional 4 Ps

The Marketing Mix is the set of controllable, tactical marketing tools that a company blends to produce the response it wants in the target market.

Product

Variety, Quality, Design, Features, Brand Name, Packaging, Services

Price

List Price, Discounts, Allowances, Payment Period, Credit Terms

Place

Channels, Coverage, Locations, Inventory, Transport

Promotion

Advertising, Sales Promotion, Public Relations, Sales Force, Direct Marketing

Modern Marketing Management (The new 4 Ps)

People

Internal and external stakeholders impacting marketing success.

Processes

The creativity, discipline, and structure brought to marketing management.

Programs

All of the firm's consumer-directed activities, encompassing the old 4 Ps.

Performance

The range of possible outcome measures, both financial and non-financial.

Historical Perspectives: Marketing Philosophies

Marketing has evolved through several distinct philosophies or concepts over time.

1. The Production Concept

Focuses on affordable and widely available products. Prioritizes high production efficiency and mass distribution. "If we make it cheap and available, they will buy it."

2. The Product Concept

Belief that consumers favor products with the highest quality, performance, or innovative features. "Build a better mousetrap, and the world will beat a path to your door."

3. The Selling Concept

Assumes consumers won't buy enough unless persuaded through aggressive sales and promotion. Focus is on selling what the company makes, not what the market wants.

4. The Marketing Concept

Focuses on understanding and meeting customer needs and wants more effectively than competitors. "Find a need and fill it." Customer satisfaction is the path to profitability.

5. Societal Marketing Concept

An extension of the marketing concept that balances customer satisfaction and company profits with the well-being of society and the environment. Focus on long-term value for all stakeholders.
