4. Promotion

What is Promotion?

Promotion refers to activities that communicate the merits of the product and persuade target customers to buy it. It's how a business communicates with customers to:

  1. Inform them about the product or service.
  2. Persuade them to buy it.
  3. Remind them to keep the brand in mind.

The Promotional Mix includes:

  • Marketing Communications
  • Personal Promotion
  • Sales Promotion
  • Advertising
  • Digital Marketing
  • Mobile Marketing
  • Branding
  • Direct Marketing

Types of Promotion Strategies

Promotional strategies can be broadly categorized based on the scope and targeting of the communication.

Above-the-Line (ATL) Promotion

Uses mass media to reach a wide, general audience. Best for building brand awareness. Examples include TV ads, radio spots, and newspaper ads.

Below-the-Line (BTL) Promotion

Uses targeted and direct communication methods to reach specific customer segments. Best for driving conversions. Examples include flyers, in-store promotions, and direct email.

Through-the-Line (TTL) Promotion

Combines both ATL and BTL strategies to create an integrated campaign. For example, a TV ad (ATL) that drives customers to a website to claim a personalized discount (BTL).

Advertising

Advertising is a way businesses communicate with customers to promote their products, services, or brand.

Types of Advertising

Advertising Styles

Ads tell a story to create an emotional connection. People remember stories better than plain facts, making the brand relatable.

Ads use humor to entertain and make the message memorable. Laughter grabs attention and makes the brand likable.

Ads evoke feelings like happiness, nostalgia, or sadness to connect with audiences. Emotional ads make people care about the brand.

Ads feature celebrities to build credibility and attract fans. Fans trust and aspire to be like celebrities, influencing their purchases.

Ads highlight risks or dangers to motivate action. Fear can drive urgency and decision-making.

Unconventional and creative methods to grab attention in public spaces. This style surprises audiences and creates buzz.

Advantages of Advertising

  • Reaches a Large Audience: Ads can reach millions of people.
  • Builds Brand Image: Helps people remember your brand.
  • Drives Sales: Encourages customers to buy.
  • Supports Other Marketing Efforts: Works with promotions or sales.

Digital Marketing

Digital marketing refers to the use of digital channels, platforms, and strategies to promote products, services, or brands to reach and engage with a target audience. Unlike traditional marketing methods, digital marketing leverages the internet, mobile devices, social media, search engines, email, and other digital tools to achieve marketing goals.
