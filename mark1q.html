Section 1: Fundamentals of Promotion (Questions 1-20)

  1. What is the primary definition of "Promotion"?

    Promotion refers to activities that communicate the merits of the product and persuade target customers to buy it.

  2. List the eight types of promotional activities mentioned.

    Marketing communications, Personal promotion, Sales promotion, Advertising, Digital marketing, Mobile marketing, Branding, and Direct marketing.

  3. What are the three core goals of promotion?

    1. Inform them about the product or service. 2. Persuade them to buy it. 3. Remind them to keep the brand in mind.

  4. Explain the goal to "Inform." When is this goal most important?

    This goal focuses on creating awareness and providing information about a new product or feature. It is most important during the introduction stage of the product life cycle.

  5. Explain the goal to "Persuade." When is this goal most important?

    This goal focuses on encouraging customers to switch brands, make a purchase, or change their perception. It is most important during the growth stage when competition increases.

  6. Explain the goal to "Remind." When is this goal most important?

    This goal focuses on maintaining brand awareness and keeping the product in the customer's mind. It is most important during the maturity stage to encourage repeat purchases.

  7. Define "Above-the-Line" (ATL) Promotion.

    ATL promotion uses mass media to reach a wide, undifferentiated audience. It is best for building broad brand awareness.

  8. Provide three examples of ATL media.

    TV ads, radio commercials, and newspapers.

  9. Define "Below-the-Line" (BTL) Promotion.

    BTL promotion uses more targeted and direct communication methods to reach specific, niche audiences. It is best for driving immediate responses and conversions.

  10. Provide three examples of BTL activities.

    Flyers, trade shows, in-store promotions, direct mail, and couponing.

  11. Define "Through-the-Line" (TTL) Promotion.

    TTL promotion combines both ATL and BTL strategies to create an integrated campaign that builds brand awareness and drives direct responses simultaneously.

  12. Provide the example of a TTL strategy given in the presentation.

    A TV ad (ATL) that drives customers to a website where they can receive a personalized discount (BTL).

  13. What is the primary objective of Digital Marketing?

    To use digital channels, platforms, and strategies to promote products, services, or brands to reach and engage with a target audience online.

  14. How does digital marketing differ from traditional marketing?

    Unlike traditional methods, digital marketing leverages the internet, mobile devices, social media, search engines, and email, allowing for greater interactivity, targeting, and measurement.

Section 2: Advertising in Detail (Questions 21-60)

  1. What is the definition of Advertising?

    Advertising is a way businesses communicate with customers to promote their products, services, or brand.

  2. What are the two main categories in the "Types of Advertising" flowchart?

    Online and Offline.

  3. List the five types of "Online" advertising shown.

    Display and Rich Media, Search Engine Marketing, Email Marketing, Social Media Marketing, and Video Advertising.

  4. List the three main types of "Offline" advertising shown.

    Print Media, Radio, and Television.

  5. What are three examples of "Print Media" advertising?

    Newspapers, Hoardings (billboards), and ads on Bus/Shelters.

  6. What is "Storytelling Advertising"?

    It is an advertising style where ads tell a story to create an emotional connection with the audience.

  7. Why is storytelling effective in advertising?

    Because people remember stories better than plain facts, which makes the brand more relatable and memorable.

  8. What is "Humor Advertising"?

    It is a style where ads use humor to entertain the audience and make the message memorable.

  9. What are the benefits of using humor in an ad?

    Laughter grabs attention and makes the brand seem more likable and approachable.

  10. What is "Emotional Advertising"?

    It is a style where ads evoke feelings like happiness, nostalgia, or sadness to connect with audiences on a deeper level.

  11. What is the goal of emotional advertising?

    To make people care about the brand and build a strong emotional bond that goes beyond the product's features.

  12. What are "Celebrity Endorsements"?

    This is a style where ads feature celebrities to promote a product.

  13. Why are celebrity endorsements effective?

    They are effective because fans often trust and aspire to be like celebrities, which can influence their purchasing decisions and build credibility for the brand.

  14. What is "Fear-Based Advertising"?

    It is a style where ads highlight risks, dangers, or negative consequences to motivate a specific action from the audience.

  15. How does fear-based advertising work?

    Fear can drive a sense of urgency and prompt immediate decision-making. For example, an ad showing the dangers of not having a smoke detector encourages people to buy one.

  16. What is "Guerrilla Advertising"?

    It is an advertising strategy that uses unconventional and creative methods to grab attention in public spaces.

  17. What is the goal of guerrilla advertising?

    The goal is to surprise audiences, create a memorable experience, and generate buzz or word-of-mouth publicity.

  18. List the four "Advantages of Advertising" mentioned.

    1. Reaches a Large Audience. 2. Builds Brand Image. 3. Drives Sales. 4. Supports Other Marketing Efforts.

  19. How does advertising help to "Build Brand Image"?

    Through repetition and consistent messaging, advertising helps people remember the brand name, logo, and what it stands for, creating a distinct identity in their minds.

  20. How does advertising "Support Other Marketing Efforts"?

    Advertising can be used to announce a sales promotion, drive traffic to a retail location, or support a new product launch, making other marketing activities more effective.
